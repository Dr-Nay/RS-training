🚨 The Operations Crossroads: MidCorp Challenge

Interactive Element: You're the COO of MidCorp Manufacturing, a $2B company facing declining margins and increasing competition. Your CEO has announced the implementation of the CPR Model. Three department heads approach you with concerns:

Sales Director: "We can't afford any disruption to our Q4 pipeline"
Production Manager: "Our efficiency metrics are already stretched thin"
HR Director: "Our teams are experiencing change fatigue"

🎯 Interactive Decision Point

How do you respond to each concern while advancing CPR implementation?

Scenario 1: Sales Director's Q4 Pipeline Concern
  • a) "CPR implementation will be paused until Q1 to protect your pipeline"
  • b) "Let's identify how CPR can actually strengthen your Q4 results"
  • c) "The CEO has mandated this, so we have no choice"
Scenario 2: Production Manager's Efficiency Concern
  • a) "We'll implement CPR in non-production areas first"
  • b) "Let's use CPR to identify efficiency improvement opportunities"
  • c) "Your concerns are noted, but we must proceed as planned"
Scenario 3: HR Director's Change Fatigue Concern
  • a) "We'll slow down the implementation timeline"
  • b) "Let's design CPR implementation to energize rather than exhaust teams"
  • c) "Change fatigue is temporary - results will motivate people"

🎯 Crisis Resolution Summary

COO Leadership Principle:

Transform Resistance into Partnership

Rather than dismissing concerns or forcing compliance, successful COOs reframe challenges as collaboration opportunities. This builds buy-in and creates stronger implementation outcomes.
